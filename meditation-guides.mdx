## Introduction to Stoic Meditation

Stoic meditation combines ancient philosophical contemplation with modern mindfulness techniques. These practices help cultivate inner peace, clarity of thought, and emotional resilience.

## Contemplation Practices

Use these guided exercises to deepen your understanding of Stoic principles through focused reflection.

<Steps>
  <Step title="View from Above" icon="globe">
    Imagine viewing your life from a cosmic perspective. This practice reduces ego and increases perspective.
    
    <CodeGroup tabs="Visualization Script">
      ```javascript
function viewFromAbove() {
  const perspectives = [
    "Neighborhood level",
    "City level", 
    "Country level",
    "Planet level",
    "Universe level"
  ];
  perspectives.forEach(level => {
    console.log(`From ${level}: How small are my concerns?`);
  });
}
      ```
    </CodeGroup>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Negative Visualization" icon="alert-triangle">
    Briefly imagine losing what you value most. This builds gratitude and prepares you for change.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Virtue Reflection" icon="star">
    Contemplate how you can embody wisdom, courage, justice, and self-control today.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Mindfulness Techniques

Integrate Stoic wisdom with mindfulness practices for enhanced emotional regulation and mental clarity.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Breath Awareness" icon="wind">
    Use breathing as an anchor for present-moment awareness while reflecting on Stoic principles.
    
    <Expandable title="Guided Breathing" default-open="false">
      As you breathe in, think "I accept what is." As you breathe out, think "I focus on virtue."
    </Expandable>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Body Scan" icon="user">
    Systematically bring awareness to different parts of your body while practicing acceptance.
    
    <Callout kind="tip">Combine body awareness with the question: "What is my role in this moment?"</Callout>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<ExpandableGroup>
  <Expandable title="Morning Meditation" default-open="true">
    Start your day with 10 minutes of Stoic contemplation. Focus on setting intentions aligned with virtue.
  </Expandable>
  <Expandable title="Evening Wind-Down" default-open="false">
    End your day by reflecting on how you lived your values. Practice forgiveness and gratitude.
  </Expandable>
  <Expandable title="Stress Response" default-open="false">
    When feeling overwhelmed, pause for a 2-minute Stoic breathing meditation to regain composure.
  </Expandable>
</ExpandableGroup>

<Callout kind="info">Regular meditation practice strengthens your ability to respond thoughtfully rather than react impulsively.</Callout>

| Meditation Type | Duration | Best Time | Focus |
|----------------|----------|-----------|-------|
| Morning contemplation | 10-15 min | Start of day | Setting intentions |
| Evening reflection | 5-10 min | End of day | Gratitude and learning |
| Stress relief | 2-5 min | As needed | Breathing and acceptance |
| Deep contemplation | 20-30 min | Weekly | Philosophical inquiry |
